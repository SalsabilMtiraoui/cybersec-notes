It's a combination or principles, security objectives like a triangle. It's the fundamentals of security, refered as the AIC TRIAD. Availability (systems and networks must be up and running all the time), Confidentiality (prevents disclosure of information) and Integrity (messages can't be modified without dtection.

- Confidentiality : Making data availible, but only to the right people is confidentiality, therefore we need encryption to make sure it's to the right person. People in the middle shouldn't decern anything important from it. Restrict ressources selectively. Two factor authetification
- Integrity : making sure the data received is exactly the data sent. For that we use hashing, hash is sent with the data of a fixed length. Digital signature : Mathematical scheme to verify the integrity data, it's a hash encryption, adding a level of integrity. Certificates combine with a digital signature to add integrity.Non-repudation : provides proof of integrity, can confirm without a doubt that the info received is from the right person. 
- Availible : Redundancy = build system to coninue even if a system fails. Fault tolerance, and patching gives stability, close security holes
